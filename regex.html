Convert a boolean search query to a regular expression!

What to type

  1. Please wrap each search term in double quotes, and capitalize operators.

    • "spam" AND "eggs"
    •  NOT "beans"
    • "tomatoes" OR "bacon" OR "potatoes"
    • "healthy" OR NOT "tasty"
    • "fried eggs" AND "toast" NOT "spam (spam, spam...)"

  2. You can use parentheses to group terms.... so long as they aren't nested.

    • ("ham" OR "bacon") AND ("spam")
    • ("tomatoes" AND "beans") OR ("spam")
    • ("eggs" AND "toast") NOT ("spam" OR "bacon")
    • ("eggs" AND ("toast" OR "bacon")) AND ("spam")

  3. If you use parentheses once, please use them everywhere.

    • "eggs" OR ("biscuits" AND "gravy")
    • ("eggs") OR ("biscuits" AND "gravy")

Try it!
